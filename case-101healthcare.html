Project Overview

101 Healthcare is a Canadian home healthcare provider offering compassionate, in-home medical and non-medical support. I designed their complete website experience from scratch, defining user journeys, wireframes, and high-fidelity designs that communicated empathy and trust.

Goal: Build a digital presence that inspires confidence and simplifies service discovery for patients and families.

Role

UX/UI Designer

Responsibilities

Research, UX Strategy, Visual Design

Timeline

8 Weeks

Users & Key Needs

The interface had to be inclusive and easy to navigate across all age groups and tech familiarity levels.

Family Decision-Makers
45–60 years

Typically children of elderly patients who research and decide on care options.

Key Needs

  • Establish trust in service quality
  • Quick access to contact information
  • Clear pricing and service details
Seniors Receiving Care
65+ years

Elderly individuals who need assistance with daily activities or medical care.

Key Needs

  • Clarity and reassurance
  • Accessible design with large text
  • Simple navigation
Working Caregivers
30–50 years

Family members balancing work responsibilities with caregiving duties.

Key Needs

  • Fast booking process
  • Transparent service details
  • Mobile-friendly experience

Challenges

Building from Scratch

Creating a complete digital experience without existing content structure or design guidelines.

Tone Balance

Balancing professional medical credibility with warm, empathetic visual communication.

Accessibility

Ensuring clarity for seniors and caregivers with varying levels of technical literacy.

Mobile-First

Designing a responsive, accessibility-friendly interface that works across all devices.

Solutions

Clear Information Architecture

Developed intuitive navigation and content hierarchy for easy scanning and discovery.

Trust-Building Visual Design

Used a calm color palette with balanced whitespace and clean, readable typography.

Empathetic Content Strategy

Created compassionate microcopy and imagery that humanized the healthcare experience.

Prominent CTAs

Added clear, accessible call-to-action buttons for inquiries and quick contact options.

Accessibility-First Approach

Optimized layout for screen readers, keyboard navigation, and color contrast requirements.

Responsive Design

Ensured seamless experience across all device sizes with mobile-first approach.

Design Process

1

Research & Discovery

Conducted stakeholder interviews and competitive analysis to understand the healthcare landscape and user needs.

Key Insights

  • Trust indicators are critical in healthcare decisions
  • Users prioritize clear service descriptions over marketing fluff
  • Accessibility needs vary significantly across user groups
2

Information Architecture

Developed sitemap and user flows to ensure logical content organization and intuitive navigation paths.

Approach

  • Grouped services by care type rather than organizational structure
  • Created multiple pathways to contact information
  • Prioritized FAQ and resource sections for quick answers
3

Wireframing & Prototyping

Created low to high-fidelity wireframes and interactive prototypes to test and refine the user experience.

Tools & Methods

  • Figma for wireframing and prototyping
  • User testing with representative participants
  • Iterative design based on feedback
4

Visual Design & Branding

Developed a visual language that balances professionalism with warmth and approachability.

Design System

  • Color palette focused on trust and calmness
  • Accessible typography scale with proper hierarchy
  • Consistent component library for development handoff

What I Learned

Designing for healthcare is about building trust first, then usability. Emotional reassurance is as crucial as clarity and flow.

Emotional Design Matters

Simplicity and calm visual tone build instant comfort for vulnerable audiences, especially in healthcare contexts.

Language Builds Trust

Empathetic, clear microcopy can significantly increase perceived credibility and user engagement.

Accessibility as Foundation

Inclusive design should be integrated from the start, not treated as an afterthought or compliance requirement.

Key Takeaway

In healthcare design, the emotional experience is part of the functional experience. A design that makes users feel safe and understood is as important as one that helps them complete tasks efficiently.
